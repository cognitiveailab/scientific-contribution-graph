[Contribution 1] Formulation of explanation as multistep entailment trees
    Contribution ID: 233297051.c0
    Description: The paper reconceives open-domain textual question-answering explanation as the construction of a directed entailment tree, where each node is a multi-premise textual entailment step that incrementally derives the hypothesis (question + answer) from known facts. This formulation separates the correctness of the derivation from its utility for users, enabling systematic evaluation of explanations independent of answer selection. By defining explanations as trees rather than isolated rationales, the work provides a high-level conceptual framework that can guide future research on explainable QA.
    Types:
      - problem_formulation: It proposes a novel way to define the explanation task for QA.
      - conceptual_framework: It introduces the entailment-tree framework that structures reasoning steps.
    Sections:
      - Introduction
      - Task Definitions

[Contribution 2] ENTAILMENTBANK: a large-scale dataset of multistep entailment trees for elementary-science QA
    Contribution ID: 233297051.c1
    Description: The authors construct ENTAILMENTBANK, the first publicly released resource that provides 1,840 manually authored entailment trees linking multiple-choice science questions (from ARC) to supporting facts drawn from the WorldTree V2 corpus. Each tree averages 7.6 nodes and 3.2 multi-premise entailment steps, making explicit intermediate conclusions and the provenance of every premise. The dataset also defines three graded explanation tasks (no-distractor, distractor, full-corpus) together with evaluation metrics, thereby establishing a new benchmark for training and assessing models that generate structured explanations.

[Contribution 4] Multi‑stage relevant‑fact retrieval pipeline for the full‑corpus explanation task
    Contribution ID: 233297051.c3
    Description: To enable entailment‑tree generation when the supporting corpus exceeds the model's token limit, the authors train two sentence‑relevance classifiers (BERT and RoBERTa) on WorldTree annotations, score every fact in the corpus, retrieve the top‑25 sentences using a RoBERTa‑trained ranker, and then rerank the top‑20 candidates from each classifier with a TensorFlow‑Ranking BERT scorer.
